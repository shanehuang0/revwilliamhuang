Thursday, August 30, 2007

Verdict and Sentence

Yesterday, a 12 person jury convicted Leon Lugo Andrade of murdering William Hsing Wei Huang with a deadly weapon. They sentenced him to life in prison. Andrade will be eligible for parole after having served 30 years (including the time he spent in jail awaiting the outcome of this case), but the D.A. believes that it is unlikely that Andrade will ever get let out, because of the brutal circumstances of the murder and the identity of the victim.

Sunday, October 29, 2006

Plea deal rejected

Andrade has rejected the plea deal of 55 years in prison for a guilty plea, so the case will be going to trial. The next pretrial setting will be on Dec. 7, 2006, and the trial itself will most likely start around April or May 2007.

Tuesday, August 29, 2006

Clarification on arraignment and press conference

The arraignment is Wednesday morning at 9 AM. Andrade himself will probably not be present, and he will probably simply be assigned a defense attorney who will prepare documents for his case. The actual trial and court dates will probably not be set for a while, but we will post them here when we know about it.

On Friday at 10AM, we will be conducting a press conference. There will also be an Officer Po Chen from HPD, in addition to my family and Rep. Martha Wong. Both were very helpful to our family throughout the entire ordeal, and we are grateful for their support.
