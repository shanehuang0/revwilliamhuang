Murder Case

STATUS on SUSPECT, LEON ANDRADE

Mar. 12 (Sunday) ~10:35pm – Leon Andrade slashed Reverend William Huang with a machete 3 times on his face and once at the back of his head - Huang died of a severe blow to the back of his head leaving a big hole in his head and a shattered skull. He also lost his thumb while trying to defend his face. The blood was found in front of the restaurant by his car, but his body was found in the alley where he was trying to escape. There were 3 eyewitnesses (the criminal’s wife and daughter whom Reverend William Huang was trying to protect and an eyewitness that pulled up during the event)

Mar. 14 (Tuesday) – it was reported that the suspect was spotted in same Houston neighborhood where he lived. Police were unsuccessful in finding him after the report was called in. “It was during traffic hour so it took too long to get there.” – Phillip J. Guerrero, Sr. Police Investigator in the Homicide Division - Chicano Squad

Mar. 15, 4pm (Wednesday) – “suspect called two different persons in Houston this morning between 1:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m. These two people then called CrimeStoppers and gave the same phone number that he was calling from. The phone number comes back to an SBC line in Dallas, but is a non-assigned phone number. I received a call from CrimeStoppers this afternoon and forwarded a GB to Dallas PD. I will e-mail his photo tomorrow morning to their Violent Offenders Task Force.” – Phillip J. Guerrero, Sr. Police Investigator in the Homicide Division - Chicano Squad

Mar. 20, 8pm (Monday) – “The suspect's van was found in Sharpsburg, Georgia, a small unincorporated town outside of Marietta. The Coweta County Sheriff's office spoke with a witness who told them that he had given the suspect a ride to the Greyhound bus terminal in Atlanta. The suspect told him that he was going to catch a bus to Asheville, North Carolina, and then catch a cab to Morganton. Both cities are small, but have small areas of Hispanics. The suspect's photo and information has been forwarded to these 2 cities. Coweta Sheriff's office is trying to get any video surveillance from Greyhound stations to see if the suspect can be seen. The Georgia Bureau of Investigation is going to be called to process the van and recover any evidence. The evidence will then be sent here to be processed. Several beer cans with labels indicating they were from Texas were found in the field. They have not entered the van and I told them to go ahead because it was considered abandoned and a search warrant was not needed. Sgt. Kennedy said that it appeared that there was some clothing in the van and they will also recover that.” - Phillip J. Guerrero, Sr. Police Investigator in the Homicide Division - Chicano Squad

Mar. 22, 9am (Wednesday) – “No update since March 20th update. No update on the evidence found in van or if weapon was found in van. We are waiting for them to contact us” - Phillip J. Guerrero, Sr. Police Investigator in the Homicide Division - Chicano Squad
